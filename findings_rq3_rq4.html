Key Findings — RQ3 & RQ4
Learner Cluster Profiles & Feedback Effects by Type · Feedback-Driven Strategic Shifts in IVR Training
RQ 3 Can distinct learner profiles be identified based on feedback responsiveness?
Post-feedback sequences were more structured (lower entropy values) and showed significantly lower transition similarity, confirming a meaningful strategic shift. K-Means clustering (k=3, silhouette=0.41) identified three distinct learner types based on ΔEntropy, ΔSuccess, latency, and Jensen-Shannon distance. The spatial separation in the t-SNE plot below confirms these are genuinely distinct behavioral profiles — not arbitrary groupings. Fast Switchers showed the greatest structural change (JS-distance = 0.38), while Stuck Learners displayed minimal behavioral change and a performance decline.

RQ 4 How do feedback effects vary across learner types?
A Mixed-Effects Model revealed a significant cluster-level interaction, indicating that feedback effects are not uniform — they are strongest for adaptive learner types. Fast Switchers showed significantly greater task success improvement (+0.14, p < .01) and larger error reduction (−0.08, p < .01) compared to Explorers and Stuck Learners. This supports the need for differentiated, profile-based feedback design in IVR training.
