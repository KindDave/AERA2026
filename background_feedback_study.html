Instructional Technology · IVR Research

Microgenetic Feedback in Immersive VR:
Behavioral Shifts & Learner Profiles

Analyzing real-time interaction logs in IVR training environments
Background & Rationale
⚠️
The Problem
Instructional feedback is critical for guiding behavioral strategies and promoting self-regulation — yet its immediate, microgenetic impact in IVR training remains underexplored.
🔍
The Gap
Most feedback research captures macro-level outcomes. The granular, moment-to-moment behavioral response to feedback — especially under cognitive load in IVR — is rarely studied.
🥽
Why IVR?
IVR provides high-fidelity simulations and precise behavioral telemetry, enabling granular analysis of learning processes through rich interaction log data.
🎯
Study Objective
Examine how learners respond to feedback in real-time within IVR, analyzing interaction logs to identify behavioral shifts and learner profiles post-feedback.
Study Purpose
🎯 Purpose of the Study
Micro-Level Behavioral Change Following In-VR Feedback
Investigating how rapidly, how profoundly, and for whom feedback reshapes behavioral pathways in immersive VR training environments.

This work presents the first design-oriented micro-window model of feedback dynamics in IVR — yielding practical timing principles for adaptive feedback system design.
Research Questions
Four Guiding Research Questions
❓
Research
Questions
RQ 1 Behavioral Timing
How does immediate in-VR feedback influence behavioral strategies within a 30–60 sec window?
RQ 2 Predictive Power
Do behavioral shifts & entropy changes predict decision quality improvements post-feedback?
RQ 3 Learner Profiles
Can distinct learner profiles be identified based on feedback responsiveness and strategic shift patterns?
RQ 4 Individual Variation
How do feedback effects on task success & error reduction vary across learner types?
Theoretical Framework
🧠
Self-Regulated Learning (SRL) Theory
Zimmerman, 2000 · Pintrich, 2004 · Winne, 2017
  • Feedback = regulatory input, not mere correction
  • Shifts learners from passive monitoring → active behavioral control
  • IVR telemetry enables real-time tracking of these strategic shifts
⚡
Adaptive Feedback Theory
Kulhavy & Wager, 1993
  • Precisely timed feedback during behavioral uncertainty is most powerful
  • Catalyzes strategic reorganization — not just knowledge gain
  • Entropy peak = empirical signature of this reorganization moment
Key Empirical Finding
📊 Signature Discovery
Entropy Peak at 38–46 sec Post-Feedback
The moment of peak behavioral uncertainty — measured via interaction log entropy — is the direct empirical signature of strategic reorganization catalyzed by precisely timed feedback.
